Learn about Flounder fish

Flounders are a group of flatfish species. They are demersal fish, found at the bottom of oceans around the world; some species will also enter estuaries. Flounder is a type of flatfish found in both saltwater and freshwater environments around the world.

Commercial Importance

Flounder is a commercially important fish and is harvested for human consumption in many parts of the world. It is known for its delicate flavor and tender texture.

Species Diversity

There are many species of flounder, including the European flounder, summer flounder, winter flounder, and Gulf flounder, among others. Each species may have slightly different characteristics and habitats

Fishing Regulations

Due to concerns about overfishing and habitat degradation, there are often regulations in place to manage flounder populations. These regulations may include catch limits, size restrictions, and closed seasons to help maintain sustainable fisheries.
