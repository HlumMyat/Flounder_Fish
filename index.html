Learn about Flounder fish

Flounders are a group of flatfish species. They are demersal fish, found at the bottom of oceans around the world; some species will also enter estuaries. Flounder is a type of flatfish found in both saltwater and freshwater environments around the world.

Physical Characteristics

Flounders have a distinct flattened body shape that enables them to lie on the ocean floor, often camouflaging with their surroundings. They have both eyes on one side of their head, which allows them to easily observe prey while remaining partially buried in the sand or mud.

Habitat

Flounders are typically found on sandy or muddy bottoms of coastal waters, although some species inhabit deeper ocean environments. They are commonly found in temperate and subtropical regions.

Diet

Flounders are carnivorous and feed on a variety of small fish, crustaceans, and other invertebrates. They use their flattened bodies and quick movements to ambush prey.
